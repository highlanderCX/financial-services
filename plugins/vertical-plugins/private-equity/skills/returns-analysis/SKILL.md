# Investment Committee Memo — Pre-IPO Secondaries

description: Draft a structured investment committee memo for minority pre-IPO secondary, primary, or SPV investments held via BVI SPC or similar fund vehicles. Synthesizes screening, diligence, unit economics, and returns work into a formal IC document. Adapted from buyout IC format — drops 100-day plan and capital structure sections, adds vehicle / structural diligence and tax sections relevant to private secondaries. Use when preparing for investment committee, writing up a secondary deal, drafting a recommendation memo, or producing LP-facing approval materials. Triggers on "write IC memo", "investment committee memo", "deal write-up", "prepare IC materials", "recommendation memo", or "secondary memo".

## Workflow

### Step 1: Gather Inputs

Collect from the user (or from prior analysis in the session):

- Company overview, business description, stage
- Latest financials (ARR / revenue, growth, GM, NDR, burn, runway)
- Funding history and current cap table summary
- Management team profile and key recent additions / departures
- Deal terms (price, valuation, share class, tranche size, discount)
- Structural terms (transfer mechanism, ROFR status, info rights, SPV terms if applicable)
- Diligence findings (commercial, financial, structural, tax)
- Returns analysis (base / upside / downside, gross and net)
- Comparable secondary transactions and listed comps

### Step 2: Draft Memo Structure

**I. Executive Summary** (1 page)
- One-line deal description: company, tranche size, vehicle, valuation, share class
- Recommendation and headline returns (Net IRR / MOIC base case)
- Top 3 reasons to do it, top 3 risks
- Conditions to closing (if conditional approval)

**II. Company Overview** (1-2 pages)
- Business description and core products
- Customer base, go-to-market, geographic footprint
- Competitive positioning vs. direct comparables (public and private)
- Management team and recent leadership changes
- Funding history — last 3 rounds with leads, valuations, and structure

**III. Industry & Market** (1 page)
- Market size and growth
- Competitive landscape — direct comps including listed companies
- Secular trends supporting the thesis
- Regulatory environment if relevant (AI safety, data localisation, export controls for AI / frontier companies)

**IV. Operating Performance** (2 pages)
- Historical revenue / ARR (3 years if available)
- Growth trajectory and cohort behaviour
- Gross margin, with compute / infra-adjusted view for AI / hosting companies
- Net dollar retention, customer concentration, top-10 customer disclosure
- Burn rate, cash runway, capital efficiency (Rule of 40, magic number where calculable)
- Quality of revenue commentary (one-time vs. recurring, contracted vs. usage-based)

**V. Investment Thesis** (1 page)
- 3-5 pillars of the thesis
- Why this entry point is attractive (discount, structural access, timing)
- Path to liquidity and expected exit window
- Comparable transactions supporting the entry valuation

**VI. Deal Terms & Vehicle** (1 page)
- Entry valuation, implied multiples (revenue, ARR)
- Share class purchased and liquidation preference implications
- Tranche size at SPC level; aggregate fund exposure to this issuer post-close
- Vehicle: SPC class, segregation, manager, administrator
- Fees and carry to LPs in the SPC
- Allocation / commitment terms with LPs
- Subscription documentation status (US sub agreement, non-US sub agreement, side letters)

**VII. Structural Diligence** (1 page)
- Transfer mechanism: direct registration, SPV layer, forward contract — and consequences for each
- ROFR / company consent status and pathway to closing
- Information rights obtained post-close (audit-quality financials, board observer access, none)
- Counterparty diligence on the seller (legitimacy of share ownership, ROFR clean, no contested interests, no undisclosed encumbrances)
- Custody and registration mechanics

**VIII. Tax & Regulatory** (1 page)
- PFIC analysis on the underlying company (almost always relevant for non-US tech holdings)
- QEF election availability and mechanics for US LPs
- Withholding considerations on exit
- BVI / Cayman / Singapore tax treatment at the fund level
- AEOI / CRS / FATCA reporting impact
- US securities-law treatment of the offering (Reg D 506(c) and Reg S as applicable)
- ERISA benefit plan investor thresholds in the SPC if relevant

**IX. Returns Analysis** (1 page)
- Base, upside, and downside scenarios with Net IRR / MOIC
- Sensitivity highlights (exit valuation × hold period is usually the dominant 2-way)
- Comparison to fund target returns
- Returns attribution (growth, multiple, discount capture, fee drag)

**X. Risk Factors** (1 page)
- Key risks ranked by severity and likelihood, with explicit mitigants:
  - Company / market risk (growth deceleration, competitive disruption, regulatory)
  - Liquidity / exit-timing risk
  - Structural risk (ROFR break, transfer challenge, share-class dispute)
  - Valuation risk (multiple compression, down round)
  - Vehicle / counterparty risk
- Deal-breaker risks if any

**XI. Recommendation**
- Proceed / Pass / Conditional Proceed
- If conditional: enumerated conditions (e.g. ROFR clearance confirmed in writing, QEF availability confirmed by tax counsel, completion of seller-side counterparty diligence)
- Next steps and timeline to close

### Step 3: Output Format

- Default: Word document (.docx) with professional formatting
- Alternative: Markdown for quick review
- Use tables for financials, deal terms, returns, and the risk register — not just prose
- Always include a one-page summary as Section I, even if the underlying memo is 15+ pages

## Important Notes

- IC memos must be factual and balanced. Present bull and bear honestly — IC members will find soft spots regardless, and credibility compounds across deals.
- **Don't soft-pedal structural risk.** Most secondaries that fail don't fail on the company — they fail on transfer mechanics, ROFR exercise, or share-class confusion. Section VII is where this memo earns its keep versus a generic growth-equity write-up.
- **Returns should always be reported net of vehicle fees to LPs**, not just gross at the underlying level. Headline gross MOIC is misleading.
- **For US investor tranches**, the tax & regulatory section is not optional. PFIC, QEF, and US securities-law treatment should each have a clear paragraph.
- Use the firm's standard memo template if provided; otherwise the structure above.
- Don't make up deal terms or seller details. If something material is missing, list it under "open items" rather than assuming.
- Financial tables should tie — revenue figures, valuation math, and returns calcs need to be internally consistent across all sections.
